Patient Risk Prediction Model

🔎 Introduction

This demo presents a model that predicts a patient’s risk of health deterioration within the next 90 days, using health data from the past 30–180 days (vitals, lab results, medications, and medical events).

⚙️ Approach

Instead of analyzing every day individually, we summarize features such as average blood pressure, recent weight changes, lab trends, and number of ER visits. These features are fed into a Gradient Boosting model, which is powerful yet interpretable for clinical applications.

📊 Metrics

  • AUROC: Measures how well the model separates high- vs low-risk patients.
  • AUPRC: Focuses on identifying true high-risk patients among rare events.
  • Calibration: Ensures predicted probabilities match observed outcomes.
  • Confusion Matrix: Simple breakdown of correct and incorrect predictions.

🌍 Factors

Global Factors

  • Frequent hospital visits → higher risk.
  • Rapid weight gain → possible fluid buildup (heart/kidney issues).
  • Declining kidney function or uncontrolled blood sugar → higher risk.
  • Stable vitals and regular medication → lower risk.

Local Factors

  • Patient A: High risk due to rising blood pressure, weight gain, worsening kidney labs.
  • Patient B: Low risk due to stable vitals, no lab declines, no hospital visits.

💡 Example Output

“Patient A has a 72% chance of deterioration within 90 days.”
